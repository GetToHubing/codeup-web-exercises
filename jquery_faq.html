Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone encompasses 2,221,766 acres.
  • There are more than 10,000 hydrothermal features in Yellowstone.
  • Yellowstone is home to more than 500 active geysers.
  • There are approximately 1,000 miles of hiking trails in Yellowstone.

Mammoth Cave National Park

  • Has the longest cave system in the world with more than 400 miles officially mapped.
  • Officially recognized as one of the largest tourist attractions in the U.S.
  • The cave system is home to more than 12 unusual species, such as the rare Albino Shrimp, Kentucky Eyeless Cave Shrimp, Southern Cave Fish, and Indiana Eyeless Crayfish.
  • Mammoth Cave is listed as one of the natural wonders

Wrangell-St. Elias National Park

  • St. Elias is home to some of the largest volcanoes in the United States and Canada.
  • Malaspina Glacier is the largest piedmont glacier in the world, with a surface area of over 1,500 square miles.
  • The park is home to the largest single wilderness area in the nation.
  • Mt. St. Elias, at 18,008 feet (5.5km), is the second highest peak in the United States.
